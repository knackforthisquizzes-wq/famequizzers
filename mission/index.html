The Mission

FameQuizzer exists to examine attention honestly — without worshiping it, and without pretending it’s harmless.

Why this exists

Fame is usually talked about in extremes. Either it’s a dream, or it’s a cautionary tale.

FameQuizzer was created to sit in the uncomfortable middle — to treat attention as a system, not a prize. Something that shapes behavior, identity, relationships, and decision-making over time.

Instead of asking “Do you want to be famous?” we ask better questions:

How do you react when people are watching?
What parts of yourself would you amplify — or hide?
What happens when attention doesn’t go away?

What we believe

Attention isn’t good or bad on its own. It’s pressure. It reveals patterns. It rewards some traits and punishes others.

Most people never get a safe space to explore how they’d handle that. FameQuizzer turns the volume up slowly, on purpose, so you can notice your reactions before they matter.

Think of it as a thought experiment with better UI.

What this is not

Not a personality diagnosis
Not a prediction of your future
Not a path to actual fame
Not a morality test

It’s a mirror. Sometimes flattering. Sometimes strange.

The point

If you leave FameQuizzer a little more curious, a little more cautious, or a little more self-aware about attention — then it worked.
